There are analogous functions called hyperbolic sine and hyperbolic cosine. As you might suspect, these functions generate points that lie on a hyperbola; specifically, for all values of \(t\), the point \(\point{\fe{\cosh}{t}}{\fe{\sinh}{t}}\) lies on the hyperbola \(x^2-y^2=1\). The area of the “shark fin” defined by the origin, \(\point{1}{0}\), and the hyperbolic arc to \(\point{\fe{\cosh}{t}}{\fe{\sinh}{t}}\) is exactly \(\frac{1}{2}t\).
