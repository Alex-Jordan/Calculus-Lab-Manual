Solution 9.8.1.0.9.1

Since \(\fe{\fd{f}}{t}=\fe{\sech^2}{t}\), \(\fe{\fd{f}}{t}\) is positive for all values of \(t\). So \(f\) is always increasing. And we can calculate

\begin{align*} \fe{f}{0}&=\fe{\tanh}{0}&\fe{\fd{f}}{0}&=\fe{\sech^2}{0}\\ &=\frac{e^0-e^{-0}}{e^0+e^{-0}}&&=\frac{2}{e^0+e^{-0}}\\ &=\frac{0}{2}&&=\frac{2}{2}\\ &=0&&=1 \end{align*}
