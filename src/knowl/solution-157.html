Solution 5.12.1.0.32.1

\begin{align*} \fe{f}{x}&=\fe{\sin}{2x}\\ &=2\fe{\sin}{x}\fe{\cos}{x}\\ \fe{\fd{f}}{x}&=2\left(\lzoo{x}{\fe{\sin}{x}}\cdot\fe{\cos}{x}+\fe{\sin}{x}\cdot\lzoo{x}{\fe{\cos}{x}}\right)\\ &=2\left(\fe{\cos}{x}\cdot\fe{\cos}{x}+\fe{\sin}{x}\cdot-\fe{\sin}{x}\right)\\ &=2\left(\fe{\cos}{x}\cdot\fe{\cos}{x}-\fe{\sin}{x}\cdot\fe{\sin}{x}\right)\\ \fe{\sd{f}}{x}&=2\left(\lzoo{x}{\fe{\cos}{x}}\cdot\fe{\cos}{x}+\fe{\cos}{x}\cdot\lzoo{x}{\fe{\cos}{x}}\right.\\ &\phantom{{}={}}\left.{}-\lzoo{x}{\fe{\sin}{x}}\cdot\fe{\sin}{x}-\fe{\sin}{x}\cdot\lzoo{x}{\fe{\sin}{x}}\right)\\ &=2\left(-\fe{\sin}{x}\cdot\fe{\cos}{x}+\fe{\cos}{x}\cdot-\fe{\sin}{x}\right.\\ &\phantom{{}={}}\left.{}-\fe{\cos}{x}\cdot\fe{\sin}{x}-\fe{\sin}{x}\cdot\fe{\cos}{x}\right)\\ &=2\left(-4\fe{\sin}{x}\fe{\cos}{x}\right)\\ &=-8\fe{\sin}{x}\fe{\cos}{x} \end{align*}
\(\fe{\fd{f}}{\pi}=2\), and \(\fe{\sd{f}}{\pi}=0\), so the tangent line to \(\fd{f}\) at \(\pi\) passes through \(\point{\pi}{2}\) and has a slope of \(0\). The equation of this line is \(y=2\).
