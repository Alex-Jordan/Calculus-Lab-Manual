Solution 5.12.1.0.22.1

\begin{align*} \fe{f}{x}&=\frac{x}{1+x}\\ \fe{\fd{h}}{x}&=\frac{1\cdot(1+x)-x\cdot1}{(1+x)^2}\\ &=\frac{1}{(1+x)^2} \end{align*}
\(\fe{f}{-2}=2\) and \(\fe{\fd{f}}{-2}=1\), so the tangent line to \(f\) at \(-2\) passes through the point \(\point{-2}{2}\) and has a slope of \(1\). The equation of this line is \(y=1(x-(-2))+2\), or just \(y=x+4\).
