The gravitational force (Newtons) between two objects of masses \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) (kg) is a function of the distance (meters) between the objects' centers of mass, \(r\). Specifically, \(\fe{F}{r}=\frac{Gm_1m_2}{r^2}\) where \(G\) is the universal gravitational constant (which is approximately \(6.7\times10^{-11}\)N m2⁄kg2.)

3

Leaving \(G\), \(m_1\), and \(m_2\) as constants, find \(\fe{\fd{F}}{r}\) after first rewriting the formula for \(F\) as a constant times a power of \(r\).

4

The sign on \(\fe{\fd{F}}{r}\) is the same regardless of the value of \(r\). What is this sign and what does it tell you about the effect on the gravitational force between two objects when the distance between the objects is changed?

5

Leaving \(G\), \(m_1\), and \(m_2\) as constants, find each of \(\fe{\fd{F}}{1.00\times10^{12}}\), \(\fe{\fd{F}}{1.01\times10^{12}}\), and \(\fe{\fd{F}}{1.02\times10^{12}}\).

6

Calculate \(\frac{\fe{F}{1.02\times10^{12}}-\fe{F}{1.00\times10^{12}}}{1.02\times10^{12}-1.00\times10^{12}}\). Which of the quantities found in Exercise 5 comes closest to this value? Draw a sketch of \(F\) and discuss why this result makes sense.
