Solution 3.5.1.0.14.1

(On level ground), at the speed of \(50\) mph, the rate at which Hanh's truck burns fuel changes relative to the speed at a rate of \(-0.006\,\frac{\sfrac{\text{gal}}{\text{mi}}}{\sfrac{\text{mi}}{\text{hr}}}\). So, if Hanh increased her speed from \(50\) mph to \(51\) mph, we'd expect the fuel consumption rate for her truck to decrease to about \(0.0294\) gal⁄mi. Conversely, if Hanh decreased her speed from \(50\) mph to \(49\) mph we'd expect to fuel consumption rate for her truck to increase to about \(0.0306\) gal⁄mi.
