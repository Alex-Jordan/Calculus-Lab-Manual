Example2.3.1

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\to3}\frac{x^2-8x+15}{x-3}&=\lim_{x\to3}\frac{(x-5)(x-3)}{x-3}\\ &=\lim_{x\to3}(x-5)&&\text{Limit Law A7}\\ &=\lim_{x\to3}x-\lim_{x\to3}5&&\text{Limit Law A2}\\ &=3-5&&\text{Limit Laws R1 and R2}\\ &=-2 \end{align*}
(Limit Law A7 , Limit Law A2 , Limit Law R1 , Limit Law R2 )

Note that the forms of the limits on either side of the first line are \(\frac{0}{0}\), but the form of the limit in the second line is no longer indeterminate. So at the second line, we can begin applying limit laws.
