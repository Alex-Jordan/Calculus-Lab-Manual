Solution 5.12.1.0.23.1

\begin{align*} \fe{K}{x}&=\frac{1+x}{2x+2}\\ &=\frac{1+x}{2(x+1)}\\ &=\frac{1}{2}&x\neq-1\\ \fe{\fd{K}}{x}&=0&x\neq-1 \end{align*}
\(\fe{K}{8}=\frac{1}{2}\) and \(\fe{\fd{K}}{8}=0\), so the tangent line to \(K\) at \(8\) passes through the point \(\point{8}{\frac{1}{2}}\) and has a slope of \(0\). The equation of this line is \(y=\frac{1}{2}\).
