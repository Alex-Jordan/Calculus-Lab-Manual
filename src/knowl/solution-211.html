Solution 8.3.1.0.1.1

First we make a diagram of the situation.

Next we account for the various quantities and rates in the problem.

Next we find an equation that relates the quantities, and use it to find the related rates equation.

\begin{align*} d^2&=300^2+h^2\\ \lzoo{t}{d^2}&=\lzoo{t}{300^2+h^2}\\ 2d\lz{d}{t}&=2h\lz{h}{t}\\ d\lz{d}{t}&=h\lz{h}{t} \end{align*}
When the balloon is \(400\) ft in the air, we can determine the values of some of the variables form the related rates equation.

Now we can substitute these values into the related rates equation and solve for \(\lz{d}{t}\).

\begin{align*} d\lz{d}{t}&=h\lz{h}{t}\\ 500\lz{d}{t}&=400(10)\\ \lz{d}{t}&=8 \end{align*}
So when the balloon is \(400\) ft in the air, the distance between the observer and the balloon is increasing at a rate of \(8\) ft⁄s.
